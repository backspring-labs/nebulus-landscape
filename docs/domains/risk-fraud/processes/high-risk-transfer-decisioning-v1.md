# High-Risk Transfer Decisioning v1

## 1. Objective

Operationally execute the controlled sequence required to intercept, evaluate, and decision payments and transfers flagged as high-risk by fraud scoring, sanctions screening, or transaction monitoring rules, ensuring that fraudulent or prohibited transfers are blocked, legitimate transfers are released with minimal customer friction, and all decisioning activity is documented to support regulatory examination, customer dispute resolution, and model performance feedback.

## 2. Scope

This process covers:
- real-time and near-real-time interception of flagged transfers
- risk data enrichment and decision context assembly
- automated policy evaluation against risk rules and thresholds
- analyst review and manual decision for cases requiring human judgment
- decision execution (approve, decline, hold) and payment release or block
- post-decision monitoring and model feedback
- exception escalation and override governance

This process does **not** own:
- payment execution rails or settlement mechanics (`domain.payments`)
- account balance or ledger management (`domain.accounts`, `domain.ledger_core`)
- fraud detection model training or deployment (`cap.fraud_rules_and_model_governance`)
- sanctions list management (`cap.sanctions_and_watchlist_screening` list curation)
- customer communication delivery (`domain.channels_experience`)
- SAR filing (covered by `proc.sar_filing_v1`)

## 3. Trigger

A payment or transfer instruction is flagged as high-risk by one or more of: real-time fraud scoring above threshold, sanctions screening potential match, transaction monitoring rule trigger, velocity or pattern exception, or manual analyst referral.

## 4. Entry Conditions

- Payment instruction has been received and is in a holdable state within the payment processing pipeline.
- Fraud scoring, sanctions screening, or transaction monitoring systems are operational.
- Risk decisioning engine is available for automated policy evaluation.
- Analyst review queue is operational if manual review is required.
- Customer and account context data is accessible for risk enrichment.

## 6. Stage-by-Stage Flow

### Stage: `stage.high_risk_transfer.transfer_intercept_and_risk_flag`

**Description:** A payment or transfer instruction moving through the payment processing pipeline is intercepted based on a risk flag generated by fraud scoring, sanctions screening, or transaction monitoring. The transfer is placed in a hold state within the payment pipeline, and a risk review event is created. The intercept must occur before settlement finality to allow for decisioning.

**Capabilities activated:** `cap.payment_and_transfer_risk_decisioning`, `cap.fraud_detection_and_scoring`, `cap.sanctions_and_watchlist_screening`

**Systems involved:** `sys.payment_processing_platform`, `sys.fraud_detection_engine`, `sys.sanctions_screening_engine`, `sys.transaction_monitoring_system`

**Participating domains:** `domain.payments`

**Decision points:**
- Which risk signal triggered the intercept (fraud score, sanctions match, TM rule, velocity)?
- Is the transfer in a holdable state (pre-settlement)?
- What is the SLA window for decisioning based on payment rail (real-time, same-day, next-day)?
- Should the transfer be auto-declined based on policy (e.g., confirmed sanctions match)?

**Risks:** `risk.decision_latency_exceeding_sla`, `risk.intercept_after_settlement_finality`, `risk.missed_intercept_on_flagged_transfer`

**Controls:** `ctrl.real_time_risk_scoring_threshold`, `ctrl.sanctions_screening_at_payment`, `ctrl.intercept_timing_validation`, `ctrl.auto_decline_policy_rules`

**Evidence generated:** Intercept event record, risk flag source and score, payment hold confirmation, SLA clock start timestamp, payment rail and settlement window

**Handoff:** Transfer held; move to risk data enrichment and context assembly.

### Stage: `stage.high_risk_transfer.risk_data_enrichment_and_context_assembly`

**Description:** The risk review event is enriched with additional context to support decisioning. Enrichment includes customer risk rating, account history, transaction velocity analysis, counterparty information, device and channel intelligence, prior alert and case history, and sanctions screening results. The enriched context is assembled into a decision package.

**Capabilities activated:** `cap.payment_and_transfer_risk_decisioning`, `cap.customer_and_entity_risk_rating`, `cap.transaction_monitoring`

**Systems involved:** `sys.risk_decisioning_engine`, `sys.customer_master`, `sys.core_banking_platform`, `sys.fraud_detection_engine`, `sys.transaction_monitoring_system`, `sys.sanctions_screening_engine`

**Decision points:**
- Is the customer risk rating current or does it require refresh?
- Are there prior alerts, cases, or SARs associated with this customer or counterparty?
- Does velocity analysis reveal patterns consistent with structuring, layering, or bust-out?
- Is counterparty information available and screened?
- Is device/channel intelligence available (for digital origination)?

**Risks:** `risk.incomplete_risk_context_at_decision`, `risk.stale_customer_risk_rating`, `risk.enrichment_latency_consuming_decision_sla`

**Controls:** `ctrl.enrichment_completeness_checklist`, `ctrl.risk_rating_currency_validation`, `ctrl.enrichment_sla_budget`

**Evidence generated:** Enriched decision package, customer risk rating, velocity analysis results, counterparty screening results, prior alert/case/SAR history, device/channel intelligence if applicable

**Handoff:** Decision package assembled; move to automated policy evaluation.

### Stage: `stage.high_risk_transfer.automated_policy_evaluation`

**Description:** The risk decisioning engine evaluates the enriched decision package against configured policy rules and thresholds. The engine produces an automated recommendation: approve, decline, or route to analyst for manual review. Straight-through decisions (auto-approve or auto-decline) are executed immediately if policy allows. Cases requiring human judgment are routed to the analyst review queue.

**Capabilities activated:** `cap.payment_and_transfer_risk_decisioning`, `cap.fraud_detection_and_scoring`

**Systems involved:** `sys.risk_decisioning_engine`, `sys.fraud_detection_engine`, `sys.alert_management_platform`

**Decision points:**
- Does the risk score fall within auto-approve, auto-decline, or manual review bands?
- Are there policy rules that mandate decline regardless of score (e.g., confirmed sanctions match)?
- Are there policy rules that mandate manual review regardless of score (e.g., wire above threshold)?
- Should the decision be stratified by payment rail (ACH, wire, RTP, P2P)?

**Risks:** `risk.approved_fraudulent_transfer`, `risk.false_decline_customer_impact`, `risk.model_drift_causing_threshold_misalignment`

**Controls:** `ctrl.real_time_risk_scoring_threshold`, `ctrl.velocity_and_pattern_rules`, `ctrl.mandatory_decline_policy_rules`, `ctrl.mandatory_review_policy_rules`, `ctrl.threshold_calibration_governance`

**Evidence generated:** Policy evaluation result, automated recommendation, rule triggers and scores, straight-through decision record if applicable, manual review routing record if applicable

**Handoff:** If auto-approve or auto-decline: move to decision execution. If manual review required: route to analyst review queue.

### Stage: `stage.high_risk_transfer.analyst_review_and_decision`

**Description:** A risk analyst reviews the enriched decision package and automated policy recommendation, applies professional judgment, and renders a final decision: approve, decline, or hold for investigation. The analyst may contact the customer for verification, consult with specialists, or request additional information before deciding. Decision authority is governed by the analyst's authorization level relative to the transfer amount and risk type.

**Capabilities activated:** `cap.payment_and_transfer_risk_decisioning`, `cap.alert_triage_and_disposition`, `cap.customer_and_entity_risk_rating`

**Systems involved:** `sys.alert_management_platform`, `sys.risk_decisioning_engine`, `sys.customer_master`, `sys.core_banking_platform`

**Decision points:**
- Does the analyst's authorization level cover this transfer amount and risk type?
- Does the enriched context support approval, decline, or further investigation?
- Should the customer be contacted for verification before deciding?
- Should the transfer be approved with conditions (e.g., reduced amount, enhanced monitoring)?
- Does the case warrant escalation to a senior analyst or manager?

**Risks:** `risk.decision_latency_exceeding_sla`, `risk.analyst_authorization_exceeded`, `risk.customer_verification_failure`, `risk.inconsistent_analyst_decisions`

**Controls:** `ctrl.analyst_decision_authority_matrix`, `ctrl.decision_sla_enforcement`, `ctrl.customer_verification_protocol`, `ctrl.decision_consistency_monitoring`

**Evidence generated:** Analyst decision record, decision rationale, customer contact record if applicable, authorization level verification, SLA compliance status

**Handoff:** Decision rendered; move to decision execution and release.

### Stage: `stage.high_risk_transfer.decision_execution_and_release`

**Description:** The final decision (approve, decline, or hold) is executed against the payment pipeline. Approved transfers are released for continued processing. Declined transfers are blocked and the originator is notified with appropriate reason codes. Held transfers are moved to an investigation queue with the payment remaining in held state until investigation concludes.

**Capabilities activated:** `cap.payment_and_transfer_risk_decisioning`

**Systems involved:** `sys.payment_processing_platform`, `sys.risk_decisioning_engine`, `sys.alert_management_platform`, `sys.core_banking_platform`

**Participating domains:** `domain.payments`

**Decision points:**
- Has the decision been rendered by an authorized party?
- Is the payment still in a holdable/actionable state?
- What reason code should accompany a decline?
- Should the customer be notified of the decision and through what channel?
- For held transfers, what is the investigation SLA before the payment must be released or declined?

**Risks:** `risk.decision_execution_failure`, `risk.payment_released_without_decision`, `risk.incorrect_reason_code`, `risk.hold_expiration_without_resolution`

**Controls:** `ctrl.decision_execution_confirmation`, `ctrl.hold_expiration_management`, `ctrl.reason_code_accuracy_validation`, `ctrl.payment_release_authorization`

**Evidence generated:** Decision execution record, payment release or block confirmation, reason code assignment, customer notification record, hold queue assignment if applicable

**Handoff:** Decision executed. Approved transfers continue through payment pipeline. Declined transfers are terminated. Held transfers enter investigation workflow.

### Stage: `stage.high_risk_transfer.post_decision_monitoring_and_feedback`

**Description:** Following decision execution, the process captures outcome data for model performance feedback and operational monitoring. Approved transfers are monitored for subsequent fraud indicators. Declined transfers are tracked for customer dispute rates and false decline analysis. Decision data is fed back to fraud detection and risk scoring models to improve future performance.

**Capabilities activated:** `cap.payment_and_transfer_risk_decisioning`, `cap.fraud_rules_and_model_governance`, `cap.transaction_monitoring`

**Systems involved:** `sys.fraud_detection_engine`, `sys.transaction_monitoring_system`, `sys.risk_decisioning_engine`, `sys.alert_management_platform`

**Decision points:**
- Did an approved transfer subsequently result in a confirmed fraud event?
- Did a declined transfer result in a customer complaint or dispute?
- Should the feedback trigger model threshold recalibration?
- Are there emerging patterns across decisions that suggest rule adjustments?

**Risks:** `risk.feedback_loop_degradation`, `risk.undetected_model_drift`, `risk.delayed_recalibration`

**Controls:** `ctrl.post_decision_feedback_loop`, `ctrl.model_performance_monitoring`, `ctrl.false_positive_and_false_negative_tracking`, `ctrl.recalibration_trigger_thresholds`

**Evidence generated:** Decision outcome tracking records, model performance metrics, false positive/false negative rates, customer dispute correlation, recalibration recommendations

**Handoff:** Feedback captured and routed to model governance. No further action required for individual transfers.

### Stage: `stage.high_risk_transfer.exception_escalation_and_override`

**Description:** When standard decisioning paths cannot resolve a transfer (e.g., conflicting signals, system unavailability, customer escalation, high-value or politically exposed person transfers), the case is escalated to senior management or a risk committee for override decision. Overrides require dual authorization and documented justification. All overrides are subject to post-decision audit review.

**Capabilities activated:** `cap.payment_and_transfer_risk_decisioning`, `cap.risk_restriction_and_offboarding_decisioning`

**Systems involved:** `sys.risk_decisioning_engine`, `sys.alert_management_platform`, `sys.case_management_system`

**Decision points:**
- Does this transfer require escalation beyond standard analyst authority?
- Is dual authorization available for the override decision?
- Is the override justification documented and defensible?
- Should the override trigger enhanced monitoring of the customer or counterparty?
- Should the override be reported to compliance or audit?

**Risks:** `risk.override_without_authorization`, `risk.override_pattern_abuse`, `risk.inadequate_override_documentation`

**Controls:** `ctrl.override_dual_authorization`, `ctrl.override_documentation_requirements`, `ctrl.override_pattern_monitoring`, `ctrl.override_audit_reporting`

**Evidence generated:** Escalation record, override decision with dual authorization, override justification narrative, enhanced monitoring flag if applicable, audit reporting record

**Handoff:** Override decision executed; transfer returns to decision execution stage for release or block. Override record available for audit review.

## 7. Decision Points

Key decisions across the process:
- Risk flag source identification and intercept timing
- Enrichment completeness assessment
- Automated policy: auto-approve vs auto-decline vs manual review
- Analyst decision: approve vs decline vs hold vs approve with conditions
- Decision authority: within analyst level vs requires escalation
- Override: authorized dual-approval vs denied
- Post-decision: model feedback triggers recalibration vs no action

## 8. Alternate Outcomes

- Transfer auto-approved by policy engine with no analyst review required
- Transfer auto-declined based on mandatory decline policy (e.g., sanctions match)
- Transfer approved by analyst after customer verification
- Transfer declined by analyst; customer disputes and outcome is reviewed
- Transfer held pending investigation; subsequently released or blocked
- Transfer escalated and approved via senior management override with conditions
- Transfer approved but triggers enhanced monitoring of customer relationship
- System unavailability forces manual fallback decisioning

## 9. Risks (process-level)

- False declines causing customer friction and relationship damage
- Approved fraudulent transfers resulting in financial loss
- Sanctions evasion through structuring or intermediary layering
- Decision latency exceeding payment rail SLA causing operational failures
- Unauthorized overrides bypassing risk controls
- Incomplete risk context leading to poorly informed decisions
- Model drift degrading scoring accuracy over time
- Feedback loop disconnection preventing model improvement

## 10. Controls (process-level)

- Real-time risk scoring thresholds with payment-rail-specific calibration
- Sanctions screening at payment initiation
- Velocity and pattern rules for structuring and layering detection
- Analyst decision authority matrix with amount and risk-type tiers
- Override dual authorization requirement
- Decision SLA enforcement with escalation triggers
- Post-decision feedback loop to detection models
- Override pattern monitoring and audit reporting

## 11. Evidence / Audit Considerations

This process generates a high-density audit trail:

- customer contact and verification records

- reason code assignments
- override records with dual authorization

- SLA compliance records across all stages

## 14. Open Questions

- Should real-time decisioning (sub-second for RTP/FedNow) be modeled as a distinct variant from near-real-time wire review?
- How should the process handle multi-leg transfers where individual legs are below threshold but aggregate exposure is high?
- Should customer verification (outbound call, push notification) be modeled as a sub-process with its own SLA governance?
- How should the override governance model adapt for different payment rails with different finality windows?

## 15. Provenance

This process is the real-time operational spine connecting risk detection to payment execution within `domain.risk_fraud`. It operates under the tightest time constraints of any risk process because payment rail SLAs (especially for real-time payments) demand sub-second to minutes-level decisioning. The process is examination-critical because examiners evaluate the institution's ability to intercept suspicious transfers, the consistency and documentation of decisioning, override governance, and the effectiveness of the feedback loop between decisions and detection model performance.
